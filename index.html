My name is Nídia Sequeira Trovão, and I am an Assistant Professor of Molecular Epidemiology at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC). My research group utilizes advanced computational tools, including phylodynamic modeling, to study the mechanisms that drive the evolution and spread of pathogens across the One Health spectrum. We also focus on building analytical capacity in genomic epidemiology globally.

Whether you are a prospective graduate student interested in applied phylodynamics, or a researcher curious to understand how your sequence data are positioned within the evolutionary history of a pathogen and its transmission dynamics, please don’t hesitate to contact me!
