My name is Nídia Sequeira Trovão and I am currently a Postdoctoral Visiting Fellow with Dr. Martha Nelson at the Fogarty International Center, studying the mechanisms that drive evolution of influenza viruses in several animal populations. I am also part of the Multinational Influenza Seasonal Mortality Study, a network with the goal to build analytical capacity in influenza research, by organizing training workshops or hosting visiting guest researchers, and establish long-term collaborations with scientist globally. Besides influenza, I am also interested in applying phylodynamic principles to other respiratory and zoonotic viruses.

For further information or if you curious to understand how your sequence data is positioned within the evolutionary history of the pathogen, please don’t hesitate to contact me!
