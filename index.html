My name is Nídia Sequeira Trovão and I am currently a Postdoctoral Visiting Fellow with Dr. Martha Nelson at the Fogarty International Center, studying the mechanisms that drive evolution of influenza viruses in several animal populations. I am also part of the Multinational Influenza Seasonal Mortality Study, a network that aims to build analytical capacity in influenza research by organizing training workshops and hosting visiting guest researchers, establishing long-term collaborations with scientist globally. Besides influenza, I am also applying phylodynamic principles to other respiratory and zoonotic viruses, such as influenza and SARS-CoV-2.

For further information or if you are curious to understand how your sequence data are positioned within the evolutionary history of the pathogen, please don’t hesitate to contact me!
